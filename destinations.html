Destinations

Zealand & The Capital

The heart of Denmark, where the capital's cosmopolitan energy meets the dramatic cliffs of the coastline.

The Capital of Cool

Copenhagen

Copenhagen is a city of contrasts where medieval spires cast shadows on sustainable glass architecture. It is a place where the concept of 'Hygge' was born and where bicycles are the kings of the road. Dive into a gastronomic wonderland boasting the world's highest concentration of Michelin stars.

The White Cliffs

Møns Klint

These dazzling white chalk cliffs rise 128 meters vertically from the turquoise Baltic Sea. Created during the Ice Age, the cliffs are a geological wonder rich in fossils. At night, it becomes a certified Dark Sky Park, offering the best stargazing in Northern Europe.

Tivoli Gardens

One of the world's oldest amusement parks, a magical oasis right in the city center.

Nyhavn

The iconic 17th-century waterfront with colorful townhouses and historic wooden ships.

CopenHill

A waste-to-energy plant with a ski slope on its roof—pure Danish innovation.

Funen — The Garden Island

The birthplace of Hans Christian Andersen, where fairytales come to life.

The Fairytale City

Odense

Stepping into Odense is like turning the page of a storybook. Matches H.C. Andersen's legacy with modern life.

H.C. Andersen House

A brand new, immersive museum designed by architect Kengo Kuma celebrating the famous author.

The Funen Village

An open-air museum reconstructing life in 19th century Denmark with authentic buildings.

Brandts

A massive cultural center for art and visual culture housed in an old textile factory.

Jutland — The Mainland

From the vibrant streets of Aarhus to the wild dunes of the West Coast, Jutland offers Denmark's most diverse landscapes.

The City of Smiles

Aarhus

Aarhus is often called the world's smallest big city. It possesses the energy of a metropolis thanks to its large student population, but maintains the intimacy of a village. Walk through three centuries at 'Den Gamle By', then see the future at ARoS Art Museum crowned with a rainbow halo.

UNESCO Wilderness

The Wadden Sea

Denmark's largest National Park is governed by the rhythm of tides. Twice a day, the ocean retreats, exposing a vast seabed. Visitors can walk on the ocean floor, harvest oysters, or witness 'Sort Sol'—millions of starlings darkening the sky.

The Capital of Children

Billund

Billund is proof that a small town can dream big. As the birthplace of the LEGO® brick, this town is the global headquarters of creativity. It is home to the original LEGOLAND® resort and the LEGO House—a massive stack of white bricks designed by Bjarke Ingels.

The Northern Tip

Skagen

At Denmark's northernmost point, two seas collide in a dramatic clash of waves. The unique light has attracted artists for centuries, and the yellow-painted houses create a palette that feels Mediterranean yet distinctly Nordic.

ARoS Art Museum

Walk inside Olafur Eliasson's "Your Rainbow Panorama" for 360° views of Aarhus in all colors.

LEGO House

The "Home of the Brick", filled with 25 million bricks and creative zones for every skill level.

Tirpitz Museum

A hidden bunker museum ingeniously integrated into the dunes of the dramatic West Coast.

The Baltic Islands

Remote gems in the Baltic Sea, where time moves slowly and nature takes center stage.

The Sunshine Island

Bornholm

Located far east in the Baltic Sea, Bornholm is a geological anomaly. Unlike the rest of the country's soft hills, Bornholm features rugged granite cliffs, deep rift valleys, and rocky coastlines. It is known for its unique light, smoked herring from iconic chimneys, and a thriving scene of ceramic artists.

Hammershus

The largest medieval castle ruin in Northern Europe, perched dramatically on the cliffs.

Dueodde Beach

Fine white sand so soft it was once used in hourglasses. The perfect summer escape.

Round Churches

Unique medieval fortified churches found only on Bornholm, blending defense and devotion.

The Kingdom — North Atlantic

Beyond the mainland: dramatic landscapes of the Realm.

The Land of Maybe

Faroe Islands

Rising out of the North Atlantic like a fortress of basalt, the Faroe Islands are a self-governing archipelago where nature rules supreme. Vertical sea cliffs drop into the churning ocean, and tiny villages with grass-roofed houses cling to green valleys.

The Arctic Giants

Greenland

Greenland operates on a scale the human mind struggles to comprehend. This is the land of the Midnight Sun and Northern Lights. The Ilulissat Icefjord, a UNESCO World Heritage site, churns out colossal icebergs that dwarf skyscrapers.

Múlafossur Waterfall

The iconic Faroese waterfall plunging into the ocean.

Sørvágsvatn

The optical illusion "lake above the ocean".

Dog Sledding

The authentic way to traverse the Greenlandic landscape.
