Destinations

Zealand & The Capital

The heart of Denmark, where the capital's cosmopolitan energy meets the dramatic cliffs of the coastline.

The Capital of Cool

Copenhagen

A city of contrasts where medieval spires cast shadows on sustainable glass architecture. Home of Hygge and world-class gastronomy.

The White Cliffs

Møns Klint

Dazzling white chalk cliffs rising 128 meters from the Baltic Sea. A certified Dark Sky Park for stargazing.

Tivoli Gardens

One of the world's oldest amusement parks, a magical oasis right in the city center.

Nyhavn

Iconic 17th-century waterfront with colorful townhouses and historic wooden ships.

CopenHill

A waste-to-energy plant with a ski slope on its roof—pure Danish innovation.

Funen — The Garden Island

The birthplace of Hans Christian Andersen, where fairytales come to life.

The Fairytale City

Odense

Stepping into Odense is like turning the page of a storybook. H.C. Andersen's legacy meets modern life.

H.C. Andersen House

Immersive museum designed by Kengo Kuma celebrating the famous author.

The Funen Village

Open-air museum reconstructing life in 19th century Denmark.

Brandts

Cultural center for art housed in an old textile factory.

Jutland — The Mainland

From the vibrant streets of Aarhus to the wild dunes of the West Coast.

The City of Smiles

Aarhus

The world's smallest big city. Walk through centuries at 'Den Gamle By', then see the future at ARoS Art Museum.

UNESCO Wilderness

The Wadden Sea

Denmark's largest National Park governed by tides. Witness 'Sort Sol'—millions of starlings darkening the sky.

The Capital of Children

Billund

Birthplace of LEGO®, home to LEGOLAND® and the LEGO House designed by Bjarke Ingels.

The Northern Tip

Skagen

Where two seas collide. The unique light has attracted artists for centuries.

ARoS Art Museum

Walk inside Olafur Eliasson's "Your Rainbow Panorama" for 360° views.

LEGO House

The "Home of the Brick" with 25 million bricks and creative zones.

Tirpitz Museum

Hidden bunker museum integrated into the dramatic West Coast dunes.

The Baltic Islands

Remote gems where time moves slowly and nature takes center stage.

The Sunshine Island

Bornholm

Rugged granite cliffs, deep rift valleys, and a thriving artisan scene. Known for smoked herring and ceramics.

Hammershus

Largest medieval castle ruin in Northern Europe.

Dueodde Beach

Fine white sand once used in hourglasses. Perfect summer escape.

Round Churches

Medieval fortified churches found only on Bornholm.

Beyond Denmark

The Danish Realm extends far into the North Atlantic. Explore the remote wonders of Greenland and the Faroe Islands.

Greenland

The world's largest island, where icebergs the size of buildings drift past colorful villages. Experience dog sledding, witness the Northern Lights, and explore Inuit culture.

  • Ilulissat Icefjord (UNESCO)
  • Traditional dog sledding
  • Northern Lights viewing

Faroe Islands

18 volcanic islands where dramatic cliffs meet grass-roofed villages. A paradise for hikers, birdwatchers, and those seeking untouched Nordic landscapes.

  • Múlafossur Waterfall hike
  • Sørvágsvatn optical illusion lake
  • Puffin watching on Mykines
