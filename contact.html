How to Reach Denmark

By Air

Copenhagen Airport (CPH) is the largest hub in Scandinavia connecting all continents. Billund (BLL) serves the west.

By Train

Hamburg to Copenhagen is a direct 4.5-hour journey. The rail network connects seamlessly with Germany and Sweden via the Øresund Bridge.

By Sea

Ferries operate daily from Norway (Oslo, Kristiansand), Sweden (Helsingborg), and Germany (Puttgarden, Rostock).

Visa Info: Denmark is a Schengen Area member. EU/EEA citizens need only a passport or ID card. Non-EU visitors should check specific requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the currency?

We use the Danish Krone (DKK), not the Euro. However, credit cards (Visa/Mastercard) are accepted almost everywhere.

Do I need to tip?

Service charge is included in the bill by law. Tipping is not expected, but rounding up for exceptional service is appreciated.

Best time to visit?

Summer (June–August) offers long days and warmth. Winter is dark but perfect for 'hygge', museums, and Christmas markets.

Can I drink tap water?

Yes, absolutely. Danish tap water is some of the cleanest and tastiest in the world. Bring a reusable bottle!

How do I get around?

Public transport (DSB trains, Metro, Buses) is efficient and punctual. Renting a bike is highly recommended for city travel.

Do people speak English?

Yes, English is widely spoken throughout Denmark, so you will have no trouble communicating.

Emergency & Practical Information

Emergency

Call 112 for police, fire, or ambulance. Operators speak English.

Healthcare

EU citizens: bring EHIC card. Non-EU: travel insurance is highly recommended.

Electricity

Type K plugs, 230V. Standard EU plugs (Type C/F) fit in most sockets.

Time Zone

CET (UTC+1) in winter, CEST (UTC+2) in summer. Same as Berlin/Paris.
