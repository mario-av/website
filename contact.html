How to Reach Denmark

By Air

Copenhagen Airport (CPH) is the largest hub in Scandinavia. Billund (BLL) serves the west.

By Train

Hamburg to Copenhagen is a direct 4.5-hour journey via the Øresund Bridge.

By Sea

Ferries operate daily from Norway, Sweden, and Germany.

Visa Info: Denmark is a Schengen Area member. EU/EEA citizens need only a passport or ID card.

Practical Information

Emergency

Call 112 for police, fire, or ambulance.

Healthcare

EU citizens: bring EHIC card. Non-EU: travel insurance recommended.

Electricity

Type K plugs, 230V. EU plugs (Type C/F) fit most sockets.

Time Zone

CET (UTC+1) in winter, CEST (UTC+2) in summer.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the currency?

Danish Krone (DKK). Credit cards are accepted almost everywhere.

Do I need to tip?

Service charge is included by law. Tipping is not expected.

Best time to visit?

Summer for long days, winter for hygge and Christmas markets.

Can I drink tap water?

Yes! Danish tap water is clean and tastes great.

How do I get around?

Trains, Metro, Buses. Renting a bike is highly recommended.

Do people speak English?

Yes, English is widely spoken throughout Denmark.
